# Belrise Industries (BELRISE) — Bull / Bear / Base Case Analysis
**Date:** May 25, 2026  
**Source:** Q4 & FY26 Investor Presentation (filed May 24, 2026)

---

## Company Snapshot
- Formerly Badve Engineering Limited; rebranded Belrise Industries
- Auto component manufacturer — chassis, exhaust, BIW, suspensions, steering columns, polymer parts
- 24 manufacturing plants across 9 Indian states + France + UK
- 38 OEMs; 74% powertrain-agnostic product mix
- Listed on BSE (544405) & NSE (BELRISE)

## Macro Context (India Auto Sector, May 2026)

### Tailwinds
- India 2W market recovering post-pandemic; premiumisation strong (≥125cc from 38% → 52% share FY19–24)
- EV transition creating new supply chain wins for agnostic suppliers like Belrise
- India Defense/Aerospace push (Make in India) accelerating order flows
- Capex cycle in auto components still early — Tier-1 suppliers benefiting from OEM outsourcing
- INR broadly stable; supports export margin from UK/France subsidiaries

### Headwinds
- Global auto demand softness (EU recession risk in 2026 continues)
- Steel/commodity input cost volatility — gross margin trend from 20.2% (FY23) to 19.2% (FY26) is a slow bleed
- Japanese OEM slowdown risk (Honda, Yamaha, Suzuki are key customers)
- EV disruption uncertainty — 2W EV ramp may compress chassis CPV if designs simplify
- US tariff risks on auto parts (indirect exposure via European subs)

## BASE CASE

**Thesis:** Steady compounder. Belrise executes its 4W/CV expansion plan, delivers 15–18% revenue CAGR over FY27–29, and aerospace/defense contributes incrementally. Margins stabilize at 12–12.5% EBITDA.

### Assumptions
- 2W segment (83% of revenue) grows at 12–14% on premiumisation + new customer wins (4 added in FY25–26)
- 4W/CV revenue grows 35–40% in FY27 (company guidance: 40–45%) — partially met
- Chester Hall (UK) and Belrise SDM (France) contribute ~₹2,000–2,500 Mn annually by FY28
- EBITDA margins hold at 12–12.5%; finance cost continues to fall with low leverage
- PAT CAGR: ~25–28% over FY27–28 on operating leverage + interest savings

### Valuation
- At current trajectory, FY27E PAT ~₹6,200–6,500 Mn → EPS ~₹7.0–7.3
- At 25–28x PE (reasonable for a quality auto ancillary): target price ₹175–205
- Cash on books (₹8,060 Mn) provides acquisition firepower / reduces risk

## BULL CASE

**Thesis:** Belrise successfully pivots from Tier-1 to Tier-0.5 system supplier, aerospace/defense scales meaningfully, and merger of BAPL + EITSPL drives EPS accretion of 15–20%.

### Key Catalysts
1. **Merger completion** — BAPL (₹14,211 Mn revenue, 5.6% PAT) + EITSPL (₹6,956 Mn revenue, 4.7% PAT) merge into listed entity at 8.3x P/E vs listed entity at 30.9x — immediate EPS dilution-proof accretion
2. **Aerospace ramp** — Chester Hall is Top-5 UK supplier to a French aircraft engine OEM; space program parts supplier. As these programs scale, high-margin aerospace revenue (EBITDA >20% sector norm) could re-rate the stock
3. **JV with Plasan Sasa (Israeli defense)** — if defense orders materialise, defense vertical could command 30–35x PE rerating
4. **4W CPV jump** — H-One India acquisition gives ₹15,000 CPV increase in 4W segment; if 4W penetration accelerates, revenue mix improves
5. **Premiumisation tailwind** — 650cc Italian OEM chassis (single source) is a small but high-margin proof of concept

### Bull Case Numbers
- FY28E Revenue: ₹1,25,000–1,35,000 Mn
- FY28E EBITDA Margin: 13–13.5% (aerospace mix improvement)
- FY28E PAT: ₹8,500–9,500 Mn → EPS ₹9.5–10.5
- Bull target PE: 30–35x (defense/aerospace re-rating)
- **Bull price target: ₹285–365**

## BEAR CASE

**Thesis:** Margin compression, integration challenges, and global auto slowdown combine to disappoint. Aerospace is too small and slow to matter. 2W EV disruption hits chassis CPV.

### Negative Triggers
1. **Gross margin bleed continues** — from 20.2% (FY23) to 19.2% (FY26); steel/commodity spike could push below 18%, compressing EBITDA margins to 10–11%
2. **2W EV simplification risk** — electric 2W chassis is structurally simpler; if OEMs in-source or design-out complexity, Belrise's core CPV could shrink 20–30% over 5 years
3. **Customer concentration** — top Indian 2W & 3W OEM is 16+ year relationship but dominates revenue mix; any market share loss by that OEM directly hurts Belrise
4. **Acquisition integration risk** — Chester Hall, BAPL, EITSPL, H-One, MagFilters, SDM all absorbed simultaneously; management bandwidth risk is real
5. **EBITDA margin history** — margins have trended DOWN every year: 13.3% (FY23) → 12.4% (FY24) → 12.3% (FY25) → 12.1% (FY26). This is not a margin expansion story YET
6. **Finance cost reduction priced in** — the big D/E improvement from 1.01x to 0.11x is already done; future PAT growth needs operating leverage, not just interest savings
7. **UK/France macro** — UK manufacturing PMI weak in 2026; Chester Hall's £18.5M revenue base is small and could face headwinds
8. **Related party transaction risks** — BAPL/EITSPL merger eliminates RPTs, which is positive, but historical RPT overhang questions corporate governance perception

### Bear Case Numbers
- FY27E Revenue: ₹1,00,000–1,05,000 Mn (miss on 4W/CV guidance)
- FY27E EBITDA Margin: 10.5–11%
- FY27E PAT: ₹4,500–5,000 Mn → EPS ₹5.1–5.6
- Bear PE: 18–20x (de-rating on margin disappointment)
- **Bear price target: ₹92–112**

## Key Things to Watch

- **Quarterly EBITDA margin trend** — any recovery above 12.3% (FY25 level) is bullish signal
- **BAPL + EITSPL merger timeline** — NCLT approval and effective date
- **Chester Hall revenue scaling** — watch for quarterly contribution disclosure
- **4W/CV % of manufacturing revenue** — currently 13.5%; guidance to grow materially in FY27
- **Defense order disclosures** — Plasan Sasa JV, Israeli + Indian OEM empanelment conversion to real orders
- **EV 2W chassis complexity** — industry watch item, not yet a crisis but a 3–5 year structural risk

---

## Bottom Line

Belrise is a well-run auto ancillary with a credible diversification story. The debt cleanup is impressive. The aerospace/defense pivot is the right long-term move but is nascent. The core risk is the slow EBITDA margin compression trend — if that reverses on operating leverage and better product mix, this is a base-to-bull story. If margins continue to drift, the de-rating from PE compression + earnings miss could be painful.
