## 🧩 Section-by-Section Breakdown

### 1. Nervous System & Money (0:01–06:47)

**Core idea:** The subconscious mind and nervous system work as one unit. Your identity (what you believe is possible, safe, and worthy) is stored in the subconscious. The nervous system then communicates with those stories — and when you get too close to "unfamiliar" success, it pumps the brakes.

**How it shows up:**
- Procrastination
- Hyper hustle (overwork as a safety mechanism)
- Overthinking, fear, doubt

**Key insight:** The #1 block isn't strategy — it's **the ability to receive**. Making money involves tactics (ads, funnels, launches). But selling, having conviction, being seen — that's a *receiving* process. If you can't receive a compliment without deflecting, you likely can't receive money without sabotaging it.

**Practical test:** How do you receive a compliment? If you minimise, deflect, or justify ("oh it was on sale"), that's your receiving capacity showing its limits.

---

### 2. The Wealth Container Framework (14:02–28:54)

Jamie's model has **5 layers** that determine your "wealth container" — how much abundance your nervous system will let you hold:

| Layer | What It Controls | Common Block |
|---|---|---|
| **Visibility** | Being seen, marketing, standing out | Hiding behind "strategy" instead of showing up authentically |
| **Loyalty** | Belonging, not outgrowing your tribe | Fear of success making you "different" from family/friends |
| **Responsibility** | Independence, control, delegation | Hyper-independence, inability to outsource or trust others |
| **Ease** | Allowing things to come without struggle | "If I didn't bleed for it, do I even deserve it?" |
| **Identity** | Who you believe you are at your core | Imposter syndrome, shrinking when witnessed |

**Most people are stuck between Responsibility and Loyalty:**
- **Responsibility types:** Do everything themselves, can't delegate, drive themselves into exhaustion. They need to learn to *receive support*.
- **Loyalty types:** Unconsciously fear outgrowing the people who raised them. Success triggers guilt ("who am I to have more than my mother?").

---

### 3. Overcoming Money Shame (28:54–37:06)

**Sources of money shame:**
1. **Personal mistakes** — bad investments, debt, decisions made from limited knowledge
2. **Inherited stories** — parents fighting about money, "we can't afford it," "rich people are greedy"

**Reframe:** You did the best you could with the knowledge you had at the time. The shame is a story — you can choose whether to keep carrying it.

**Money is neutral.** It only amplifies your existing value system. A generous person with money becomes *more* generous. A greedy person with money becomes *more* greedy.

> "I don't understand how you have success and you don't scam people" — Jamie's father. His story was that wealthy = bad. Her job was to prove that both can coexist.

---

### 4. Human Design in Business (32:07–37:06)

Jamie integrates **Human Design** with nervous system work to help clients avoid burnout.

**Key types discussed:**
- **Generators:** Meant to only say YES to full-body yeses. Decision comes from the sacral (gut) — move forward or move back. No "hell maybe."
- **Projectors** (Jamie's type): See patterns others miss. Think in systems. Best as advisors/guides.

**Real result:** A generator client with lifelong colitis — gone within 4 months of learning to make decisions from her body instead of her head.

**The takeaway:** Strategy only runs ~5% of your business. Everything else is *you* — your internal state, how you design your energy, how you make decisions.

---

### 5. Fear of Visibility (37:06–44:43)

The biggest emotional block for most people (especially women): **being seen.**

**What it looks like:**
- Behind the camera: funny, quirky, relaxed. On camera: performative, scripted, "20 takes" trying to be perfect.
- Following "rules" instead of being authentic
- Hiding behind content volume ("I post 3x a day") without truly revealing themselves

**The work:** Can you hold your own power when someone projects onto you? If a comment hurts, it's revealing a belief you hold about yourself. The sting = a mirror.

**Jamie's strategy:** "Blinders up." She never scrolls competitors. Whatever you absorb imprints as truth in your nervous system.

---

### 6. Stop Strategy Hopping (44:43–57:07)

**Why people hop strategies:**
Strategy provides *temporary nervous system regulation* — "if I follow the rules, I'm safe." But they don't give it enough time to work, then jump to the next thing.

**The noise problem:** "This marketing is dead. Don't do it this way. This funnel is wrong." It's massively disregulating to the body.

**The fix:** Understand your design first. Then pick marketing that feels natural — maybe it's Substack, podcasting, car talks, YouTube. If you're drowning in noise, you'll never find what works *for you*.

**Two things that matter when launching:**
1. **Tactical:** Reverse-engineer from where you want people to go. Start with the CTA, then riff backwards.
2. **Internal:** Trust your design's natural pattern. For Jamie (Projector): find the problem, see the pattern, give the solution. That's enough.

---

### 7. Reprogramming Your Inner Critic & Enoughness (57:07–1:08:13)

**The goal-setting trap:**
When a goal doesn't hit, ask: *What emotional state was I hoping it would give me?* Confidence? Safety? Worthiness?

Then give yourself that state *regardless* of the outcome.

**Scarcity vs. Abundance in action:**

| Scarcity Mindset | Abundance Mindset |
|---|---|
| "This HAS to work or I'm screwed" | "I'm creating this from expansion" |
| Strategy-heavy, over-consumes, outsources trust | Deep resilience, neutrality around outcomes |
| Even if it works → fear of the other shoe dropping | If it doesn't work → curiosity, data gathering, try again |
| Burnout is inevitable | Sustainable by design |

**The practice:** Find abundance *already present* — running water, trees, a child's eyes, an apple in the fridge. You can't create momentum from survival brain. Small moments of noticing → body relaxes → action becomes powerful.

---

### 8. Three Truths & Greatness (1:08:13–end)

The closing segment where Jamie shares her three truths about living a great life — centered around self-trust, presence, and the journey being the point (not the destination).
