## Janmashtami Design Philosophy
For Krishna idol dressing (poshak/vastra) and temple decorations:
- **Color Palette**: 
  - Pitambar (Yellow): Krishna's traditional attire
  - Neelam (Blue): Divine color representing infinity
  - Gulabi/Red: For Radha's attire and festive accents
  - Swet (White): Purity and peace
  - Sona (Gold): Divinity and prosperity
- **Key Motifs**:
  - Peacock Feather (Mor Pankh): Krishna's favorite adornment
  - Flute (Murali): Symbol of divine music
  - Lotus (Kamal): Purity and spiritual awakening
  - Butter Pot (Makhan Matki): Krishna's childhood love
  - Cow Footprints (Gaupad): Symbol of Govardhan Leela
  - Mukut (Crown): Royal divinity
  - Banana Leaf: Auspicious offering
- **Pattern Styles**:
  - Traditional Butis (small repeated motifs)
  - Floral Vines and Creepers
  - Geometric Temple Patterns
  - Mythological Scene Borders
  - Zari and Thread Work Emphasis
