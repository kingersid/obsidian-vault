# Obsidian Vault Schema

## Overview
This document explains the folder structure and organizational schema for this Obsidian vault. The vault is organized into distinct areas to support different types of information management and workflows.

## Folder Structure

### 📁 `wiki/` - Durable Knowledge
**Purpose:** Permanent, structured knowledge base for reference and research.

**Contents:**
- Documented processes and procedures
- Research findings and reference materials
- Technical documentation and guides
- Project documentation and specs
- Important meeting notes and decisions
- Personal knowledge management system

**Characteristics:**
- Content is reviewed and curated
- Organized by topic and domain
- Cross-linked and inter-connected
- Long-term retention value

---

### 📁 `raw/` - Captures
**Purpose:** Raw, unprocessed data and temporary captures for future organization.

**Contents:**
- Scratch notes and random thoughts
- Meeting transcripts and audio recordings
- Draft articles and unstructured ideas
- Research raw data and sources
- Temporary project files
- Quick captures from various contexts

**Characteristics:**
- Unprocessed and unstructured
- Temporary holding area
- Regular cleanup and review
- Source material for wiki organization

---

### 📁 `output/` - Drafts
**Purpose:** Working drafts and intermediate content that's being developed.

**Contents:**
- Article drafts in progress
- Project development files
- Creative writing and brainstorming
- Document revisions
- Template variations
- Experimentation and prototyping

**Characteristics:**
- Work-in-progress content
- Iterative development
- Regular updates and revisions
- Polishing and refinement stage

---

### 📁 `daily/` - Daily Notes
**Purpose:** Time-based, dated notes for daily activities and reflections.

**Contents:**
- Daily journal entries
- Meeting notes by date
- Daily task tracking
- Thought of the day
- Progress updates
- Time-sensitive information

**Format:**
- Organized by date (YYYY-MM-DD)
- Temporal organization
- Retrospective value
- Context for current work

---

## Special Files

### 📄 `log.md` - Action Log
**Purpose:** Chronological log of all actions, decisions, and important events.

**Contents:**
- Action items and completed tasks
- Decision logs and rationale
- System changes and updates
- Important milestones
- Performance metrics
- Problem resolution notes

**Characteristics:**
- Chronologically ordered
- Permanent record of activity
- Reference for accountability
- Historical timeline

---

## Workflow Process

### Information Flow
1. **Capture** → `raw/` (immediate collection)
2. **Review/Refine** → `output/` (development stage)
3. **Finalize** → `wiki/` (permanent knowledge)
4. **Log** → `log.md` (permanent record)

### Daily Management
- **Daily Notes:** Create in `daily/` folder with date format
- **Raw Capture:** Drop unstructured content in `raw/`
- **Development Work:** Create drafts in `output/`
- **Knowledge Building:** Refine and organize into `wiki/`

### Maintenance
- Regular review and cleanup of `raw/` folder
- Archive completed drafts from `output/` to `wiki/`
- Maintain consistent naming conventions
- Cross-link related content across folders

---

## Best Practices

### Naming Conventions
- Use descriptive, consistent filenames
- Include dates for temporal content
- Use kebab-case for files
- Group related content in subdirectories

### Cross-Linking
- Link from daily notes to relevant wiki content
- Connect raw captures to developed outputs
- Reference log entries in documentation
- Maintain bidirectional links where appropriate

### Content Lifecycle
- **Raw:** 0-7 days (temporary holding)
- **Output:** 1-4 weeks (development stage)
- **Wiki:** Permanent (knowledge base)
- **Daily:** Archive monthly or quarterly
