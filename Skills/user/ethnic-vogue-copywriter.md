## Brand Voice

### The Feeling
Ethnic Vogue speaks like a trusted friend who truly loves clothes — warm, a little poetic, never salesy. The copy should make a woman feel *seen*, not sold to.

### Tone Pillars
- **Silhouette-proud**: Celebrate the cut and the shape. A well-designed kurta does something for a woman's confidence — name that.
- **Fabric-honest**: Pure fabrics are a promise. Call them out warmly — not as specs, but as feelings.
- **Detail-focused**: The hand-finishing is the craft. Draw attention to it like pointing something out to a friend — "dekho, yeh border haath se laga hai."
- **Occasion-grounded**: Anchor to real life — kitty parties, Sunday brunches, pooja at home, weddings, casual Fridays.
- **Feeling-first**: Lead with how it feels on the body. Fabric facts come after.
- **Hinglish warmth**: Mix Hindi words naturally — never forced, always warm. Think how a Surat woman talks to her bestie.

### Words We Love
*flowy, breathable, pure fabric, hand-detailed, latest cut, dupatta included, effortless, current silhouette, finishing, every-body, all-day wear, twirl-worthy, compliment magnet, pehno aur niklo, apna wala look, drapes beautifully, haath ka kaam, aaj ka look*

### Words We Avoid
*cheap, affordable, budget, good quality, nice, pretty, stylish* (show don't tell), *limited time offer*, aggressive urgency, overly historical or heritage-heavy framing

## Product Title Formula

**Format**: `[Poetic Hindi/Urdu word evoking the mood] — [Fabric] [Silhouette]`

**Examples**:
- `Roshni — Mul Cotton Straight Suit Set`
- `Khwaab — Soft Crepe Anarkali with Dupatta`
- `Sehri — Handloom Co-ord Set`
- `Baarish — Flowy Crepe Palazzo Suit`
- `Neeli Shaam — Cotton Layered Hem Kurta Set`

**Rules**:
- Title case always
- Poetic word should evoke the *mood or moment* — not just describe the print
- Keep under 60 characters for SEO
- Never start with lowercase
- If the suit includes a dupatta, mention it in the title or subtitle

## Product Description Template

Write in this order — 3–5 short paragraphs or a flowing block:

### 1. The Hook (1–2 sentences)
Open with a feeling or a scene. Make her see herself in it.

> *"Kuch outfits hote hain jisme tum comfortable bhi lagti ho aur put-together bhi."*
> *"Woh look jab kuch special lagta hai — bina kuch extra kiye."*

### 2. The Silhouette & Design (2–3 sentences)
Describe the cut, proportions, what makes this shape flattering or current. Straight fit, A-line flare, layered hem, palazzo width — be specific.

### 3. The Fabric Truth (1–2 sentences)
Name the fabric and describe how it *feels and behaves*. Drape, breathability, weight.

### 4. The Hand Detail (1–2 sentences)
Call out the finishing — what is hand-applied or hand-stitched. Make it sound worth noticing.

### 5. The Dupatta Moment (1 sentence)
Always mention the dupatta. Make it feel like a gift, not a footnote.

### 6. The Occasion Fit (1–2 sentences)
Name 2–3 real occasions. Be specific.

### 7. The Sign-off (1 line)
A soft closer — a feeling or gentle nudge.

## Hand-Detail Vocabulary

Use these naturally when describing finishing:
- **Gota work** — flat metallic ribbon appliqué, adds shimmer without weight
- **Mirror work** — hand-applied tiny mirrors, catches light, festive without being heavy
- **Thread work** — embroidered accents on yokes, hemlines, or sleeves
- **Hand-stitched border** — applied by hand, more precise and considered than machine finish

## Process for Each Copy Request

1. **Identify the content type** — product title, description, homepage, caption, category
2. **Note the product details** — fabric, silhouette, hand-detail, colour, dupatta included?
3. **Lead with silhouette and feeling** — what shape is this? What does it do for the wearer?
4. **Call out the fabric honestly** — name it, describe how it feels and behaves
5. **Spotlight the hand detail** — make it sound worth noticing
6. **Always mention the dupatta** if included — never a footnote
7. **Choose 2–3 real occasions** that feel natural
8. **End warm** — a Hindi signoff or gentle nudge
9. **Weave in SEO keywords** naturally — never stuffed

## What to Ask If Details Are Missing

If the user gives a product without enough detail, ask:
- What fabric is it? (Mul cotton, crepe, handloom?)
- What silhouette? (straight, anarkali, palazzo set, co-ord?)
- What hand-detail / finishing? (gota, mirror, thread work, border?)
- Does it come with a dupatta?
- Any specific colour or print?
- Occasion in mind?
