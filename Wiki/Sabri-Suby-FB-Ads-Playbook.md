# Sabri Suby FB Ads Playbook

Sabri Suby's framework for writing **hyper-dopamine Facebook/Meta ads** — the creative and copywriting layer that he argues outweighs media buying, targeting, and account structure. Core thesis: *stop obsessing over algorithms and targeting; obsess over people, psychology, and what makes them click.* A mediocre media buyer with a dynamite ad beats a world-class media buyer with a bland ad every time.

The video is evergreen in the *psychological* layer (which doesn't expire like platform mechanics). Applied across [[Chandni Silk Mills LLP]] (Click-to-WhatsApp B2B) and [[Label Ethnic Vogue]] (Instagram → WooCommerce), the patterns below convert directly into ad angles.

---

## 1. The Market Awareness Pyramid

In any market, demand is distributed:

| Segment | % | State |
|---|---|---|
| Buyers now | 3% | Ready to purchase right now |
| Info-gatherers | 17% | Actively researching |
| Problem-aware | 20% | Aware they have a problem |
| Unaware | 60% | Not yet problem-aware |

**Why Facebook wins:** it lets you push a message in front of the *entire* pyramid — including the 60% who aren't thinking about your category yet. Your job is to articulate a problem they're experiencing (or may experience), then warm/nurture/educate them through a funnel until they're ready, willing, and able to buy.

> Don't obsess over algorithms, targeting, or account structure. Obsess over **people** — human psychology, what gets them to click and engage. Those factors always far outweigh the media buy.

---

## 2. Steal From What Already Wins (The Transparency Report)

Facebook's own **Widely Viewed Content Report** (Transparency Center) shows the most-viewed pages on the platform every quarter. They are almost entirely **news / gossip**:

- LADbible — ~126M views
- UNILAD — ~117M
- E! News — ~116M
- People Magazine — ~109M
- TMZ — ~89M

**Implication:** people go to Facebook to consume news, gossip, and curiosity-driven content. Your ads must *feel* like that content. "Don't hate the player, hate the game" — you're not changing human psychology, you're tapping into the existing jet stream of behavior. Model your creative on LADbible, UNILAD, E! News, TMZ, Yahoo OMG, US Magazine, Wonderwall.

Same principle holds for supermarket gossip-magazine covers (decades-old proof the psychology isn't new): punchy intrigue, "6 bad habits that are healthy," "secret way to find…," "X reveals tell-all."

---

## 3. The Two Jobs of Every Ad

1. **Deliver news, value, or findings** — that's what people click on; your ad must engineer one (ideally all three).
2. **Sell the click** — bake in so much curiosity that CTR beats every other advertiser. High CTR → Meta rewards you with the best inventory and lowest CPMs.

**Curiosity is the single most powerful emotion for driving a click.** Stoke the primal monkey-brain's urge to close the intrigue gap. Other click-drivers: intrigue, shock, direct benefit, implied benefit, fear, vanity, self-interest (better/richer/stronger/faster/healthier/happier/sexier/fitter/smarter).

---

## 4. The Hyper-Dopamine Ad Formula

> **Hyper-Dopamine Ad = Clickbait (burning intrigue) + Targeted Big Benefit**

- **Clickbait alone (blind intrigue)** → fails. "You won't believe what Ivanka Trump does in the morning" attracts everyone and tells the algorithm nothing about who to target. You starve the machine of signal.
- **Benefit alone (no intrigue)** → fails. An ad nobody wants to click gets penalized by the platform.
- **The intersection** → an ad people literally can't help but click, *and* the algorithm knows who to show it to.

**Anatomy (check all boxes):**
1. **Pattern-interrupt image** — scroll-stopping, weird, native-looking (not a polished stock photo). Stares into the soul / mysterious black background / odd object in hand.
2. **Eyeball-grabbing headline** — burning intrigue + big specific benefit ("This client getting hacked feels illegal").
3. **Slippery lead-in copy** — the most *overlooked* part. Changing only the lead-in has 5x'd ROAS and dropped CPL/CPA 5x. Example: "Watch carefully, then click."
4. **Intriguing link description** — don't waste it; make it curious.
5. **Body copy** — up to **2,200 characters** so it runs on both Facebook and Instagram placements from one set of copy.
6. **Non-threatening CTA** — "Learn More" beats "Buy Now" / "Inquire Now" / "Shop Now." Sell the click, not the product.

**Test to find the bull's-eye:** form a hypothesis of what the market wants, run different angles as separate ads, and let performance reveal the winner. Uncertainty is resolved by testing, not by guessing.

## 5. Creative Styles (swipeable templates)

| Style | What it is | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **Raw native** | iPhone photo, unpolished, looks like user content. Multi-person shots do well (algorithm likes them, looks native) | Universal — Sabri's #1 go-to |
| **SMS / message style** | Mock a text/notification from a relevant sender (bank, partner, alert) | Pattern interrupt |
| **Breaking news** | "Shark Tank investor breaks silence on…" — Sabri's most reliable safe bet | Universal — #2 go-to |
| **Native highlight** | Native photo + a secondary image with red circle/arrow | Adds intrigue, stops boredom |
| **Native social post** | Mimics the LADbible/E!/TMZ post format exactly | News-feel |
| **Secret info** | "Revealed: the secret suburb set to boom" + map/visual | Info-offer leads |

**Two-image rule:** primary + secondary image stops the brain from getting bored and forces comprehension of two things — which makes them read the headline. Don't reinvent the wheel; deploy what's already proven.

**The swipe method:** never start with a blank canvas. Go study Yahoo OMG, TMZ, E! Online, People, US Magazine, Wonderwall, Gawker, Zimbio. Take their proven headlines and swap the subject to your niche:
- "Taylor Swift fans go wild after spotting X-rated detail" → "Aussies go wild after spotting this home-loan hack"
- "iPhone users advised to do one thing to stop battery drain" → "CEOs advised to do one thing before going home"
- "Secret way to find who called you without Google" → "Secret way to find $5M of business funding without banks"

## 6. Copywriting Rules

- **Readability = conversion.** Run copy through the **Hemingway app**. Best-selling NYT books and the largest YouTube channels write at a **5th-grade level or below**; Sabri aims for grade **3–4**. Consumption precedes conversion — the easier it is to read, the more read it, the more convert. You want readers *moved by the message*, not *noticing your writing*.
- **Small words, short sentences, short paragraphs.** Use one- and two-syllable words. Lots of line breaks (80–90% read on mobile). Line-by-line copy gives eye relief vs. a wall of text.
- **Sell the click, not the product.** The ad's job is the click; the funnel closes the sale.
- **Write to ONE person ("you"), but don't over-use "you"/"your."** Meta dislikes copy that calls out individuals ("not a good user experience"). Write as if to a single reader, but avoid peppering the words you/your.
- **Specificity = believability.** "Lose 12–19 kilos in 5 weeks" beats "lose weight." "Generate 90–480 inbound leads a month" beats "explode your leads." Vague claims trigger doubt; specific claims add depth and credibility.
- **Add personality and zest.** Generalized, brandy copy kills CPL. Show a little humanity.
- **Long copy outperforms short — when interesting.** Don't write long for its own sake; write long *and* compelling. Buyers read. 2,200-char cap is the FB+IG cross-placement limit — write freely, then aggressively edit down.
- **Positive hooks beat negative 8/10 times.** "Lose 20–50 kg with this one weird hack" out-pulls "Don't put on 50 kilos this winter." Test both in pain-heavy markets, but the big-benefit angle usually wins.
- **Theme everything around a winning angle.** Once a headline wins, align creative, lead-in, and body to that single angle. Send one greased slope all the way through.
- **Don't let ads look like ads.** Native platforms; people run from ads. Make it look like content.

**The only metric that matters for copy:** does it spark a comment, a share, a click? You're writing for the machine *and* the human. If it's not entertaining or valuable (news/findings), nobody consumes it.

## 8. Key Principles (checklist)

1. **Obsess over people, not algorithms.** Creative + psychology > media buying.
2. **Model ads on news/gossip** — that's what wins attention on the platform.
3. **Hyper-dopamine = clickbait + benefit.** Never one without the other.
4. **Curiosity is the strongest click emotion.** Close the intrigue gap with the click.
5. **Slippery lead-in and link description are the most overlooked levers** — they've 5x'd ROAS alone.
6. **Grade 3–4 readability.** Consumption precedes conversion.
7. **Specificity builds believability.** Numbers beat adjectives.
8. **"Learn More" CTA.** Non-threatening beats "Buy Now."
9. **Positive benefit hooks win 8/10.** Test negatives, but default positive.
10. **Long + interesting copy wins.** Don't fear the 2,200-char limit.
11. **Test angles to find the bull's-eye.** Let performance decide, don't guess.
12. **Never start blank.** Swipe from proven gossip/news headlines; swap the subject.
