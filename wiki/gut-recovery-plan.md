# Gut Recovery Plan

**Context**: Post-illness recovery (May 28 diarrhea, May 29 fever, 101 °F) — first significant illness in 6–7 years. Goal: restore healthy gut bacteria through diet, no antibiotics unless prescribed.  
**Diet**: Vegetarian  
**Start date**: May 30, 2026 (Day 1)  
**Duration**: 4 phases over approximately 4–6 weeks  

---

## Core Principles

### 1. No Antibiotics Route
Antibiotics wipe out gut flora indiscriminately. The strategy here is selective fermentation: introduce gentle, prebiotic + probiotic sources in stages so the gut can repopulate without being overwhelmed.

### 2. Reintroduction Sequence
- **Phase 1** (Days 1–3): Clear liquids → water, coconut water, electrolytes, ginger water. Let gut rest.
- **Phase 2** (Days 4–7): Very light cooked foods — rice water, moong dal soup, curd rice. Add first probiotics.
- **Phase 3** (Days 8–14): Add fermented foods, increase fiber gradually, introduce plant-based probiotics.
- **Phase 4** (Days 15+): Full gut-friendly diet — beans, whole grains, kefir/curd, fermented foods. Maintenance phase.

### 3. Key Vegetarian Gut-Friendly Foods

**Prebiotics** (food for good bacteria):
- Banana (slightly green has more resistant starch)
- Onion, garlic (cooked to avoid gas initially)
- Oats
- Flaxseed (ground)
- Jeera (cumin) — aids digestion, carminative
- Saunf (fennel) — reduces bloating
- Warm water with ginger

**Probiotics** (actual bacteria):
- Curd / dahi (fresh, homemade or good-quality)
- Buttermilk (chaas) — diluted curd with roasted cumin + salt
- Idli / dosa batter (fermented)
- Homemade pickles (limited, as gut is sensitive)
- Kefir (if available)
- Fermented rice (pancake style) — mildly probiotic

**Gut-soothing, easy to digest**:
- Moong dal (yellow, skinned)
- Rice (white rice easier than brown during recovery)
- Bottle gourd (lauki) — cooling, hydrating
- Cooked vegetables (steamed, not raw)
- Khichdi (rice + moong dal + mild spices)
- Applesauce / stewed apple (pectin is prebiotic)
- Papaya — contains digestive enzymes

**Anti-inflammatory / healing**:
- Turmeric (haldi) milk or golden paste
- Warm ginger water
- Coconut water (electrolytes)
- Sabja / basil seeds (soaked, cooling)

**Avoid during recovery**:
- Raw salads, uncooked vegetables
- Heavy dal (toor, chana, rajma)
- Fried foods
- Spicy street food / masala overload
- Sugary drinks (except coconut water)
- Dairy in excess (stick to curd/buttermilk, avoid heavy ghee/cheese initially)
- Coffee / excessive tea (limit to 1 mild cup)

**Focus**: Full vegetarian gut-friendly diet, daily probiotic, fiber variety

- Homemade curd fresh batch

**Maintenance foods to include regularly**:
- Banana (especially slightly green)
- Papaya (digestive enzymes)
- Lauki / bottle gourd

- Oats (with warm milk or water)

## Ayurvedic / Indian-Style Gut Healing Additions

- **Ajwain (carom seeds) tea**: 1 tsp ajwain boiled in water, sip after meals — reduces gas, aids digestion
- **Saunf water**: 1 tsp saunf soaked overnight in water, drink first thing — reduces bloating
- **Jeera water**: roasted jeera powder in warm water — digestive stimulant
- **Ginger-lemon-honey tea**: 1 inch ginger + few drops lemon + 1 tsp honey in warm water — anti-inflammatory
- **Haldi doodh**: warm milk + pinch turmeric + pinch black pepper (piperine helps absorb curcumin) before bed
- **Amla**: if available, small piece amla or amla juice — high vitamin C, gut lining support

---

## Hygiene & Lifestyle Additions

- **Handwashing**: soap + water before every meal and after toilet use (essential during/after illness)
- **Warm water only**: avoid cold water with meals; warm water aids digestion
- **Regular meal times**: eat at similar times daily to reestablish gut rhythm
- **Walk after eating**: 5–10 min slow walk post-meals (ties in with existing walking habit)
- **Avoid overeating**: smaller portions more often until gut fully recovers
- **Sleep schedule**: gradually shift to earlier bedtime (target 11 PM → 10 PM) as recovery progresses

---

## Red Flags (when to see doctor)

- Diarrhea returning or worsening after Day 3
- Blood in stool
- Fever returning above 100 °F after Day 2
- Severe abdominal pain or bloating that doesn't resolve
- Persistent vomiting
- Dehydration signs (very dark urine, dizziness)

If antibiotics are prescribed by doctor, continue with this diet as post-antibiotic gut rehab — just extend Phase 3–4 to 6+ weeks.
